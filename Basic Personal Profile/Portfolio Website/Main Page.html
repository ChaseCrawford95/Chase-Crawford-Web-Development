A little bit about me

I was born on May 29th, 1995, in Birmingham, Alabama. I was raised in Leeds, in the same state. After I graduated high school from Pelham, I signed a six-year contract and enlisted in the United States Air Force. At the end of my contract, I decided to leave the USAF and try to obtain a BSN in Nursing. I got into the nursing program at the University of Alabama at Birmingham but soon found out it was not for me. My reasoning for choosing a STEM degree was from one lecture in nursing school where they were discussing the subject of caring for patients that have prosthetic limbs. My mind drifted and focused on making a more technologically advanced prosthetic arm/hand. The idea landed on creating a prosthetic arm/hand that picks up the synaptic pulse signals from the body's neurological system. Then with those biological signals, send the correct inputs to the prosthetic arm/hand to make it move the way the user wants it to. I self-reflected and realized that it would be best for me to go into the STEM world if I were going to think of ideas like that. I choose Computer Science, and I can conceptualize and compartmentalize the subject material. Kind of figures coming from the son of a civil engineer.
